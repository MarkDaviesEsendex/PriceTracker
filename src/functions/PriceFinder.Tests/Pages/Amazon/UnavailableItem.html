Product Description

We’re going to make this Christmas the best one yet with our Christmas Advent Calendar 2018 gift set inspired by Great British Ales. Yep, that’s a whopping 24 craft beer selection in the 24 days running up to Christmas, each hidden behind a numbered door in our festive Christmas gift hamper. Are you on the hunt for an adult advent calendar for men or women or even just a booze-filled alcoholic advent calendar to brighten up your own Christmas countdown? Look no further, you’ve found it!

A fun beer hamper and Christmas gift set for men and women alike, with this festive beer selection you’re sure to finish 2018 in a good mood. Each beer has been carefully selected from some of the very best modern British breweries including BrewDog, Magic Rock, Siren and Wylam. We’ve picked out our all-time British beer favourites for this case. It’s an all-rolled-into-one 2018 advent calendar experience and beer hamper as well as a great chance to taste a whole range of different craft beer styles from lagers to IPAs, pale ales and silky dark beers. Treat yourself or a friend to this gift set for men and women, discover new favourites along the way, and have a very Hoppy Christmas!

3.0 out of 5 stars"24 Amazing Craft Beers"? Not quite...
12 January 2018
Verified Purchase
The advent calendar had a decent variety in terms of strength (4.0% to 8.4%), tastes (pale ales, porters, lagers, a cherry beer and the now-infamous gose or "sour beer") and locations (some imports from America, Germany, Belgium and Japan). Each selection was a standard can or small bottle (e.g. 330ml or 355ml), enough for a decent tasting.

A number of customers for this calendar were tracking their day-to-day opinions on Untapp'd, a beer review website. Most of the beers had a respectable lifetime score of 70-75%, four were poorly received and scored 60-65%, and the Gose scored 55% - including two people sharing photos tipping it down the sink!

The calendar itself was very sturdy - with a lot of cardboard packing (inside and outside) to ensure safe arrival. Pretty much everything could be recycled, so a positive from an environmental standpoint.

The one thing that surprised me with this calendar (and the main reason for the lower rating) is that despite being advertised as a "Premium Craft Beer" calendar, a third of the beers fell into one of two categories:
1) commonly available in supermarkets - for half the price
2) are brewed by multinational companies such as AB Inbev (Budweiser, Stella Artois)

From Untapp'd, I spotted six that were brewed by AB Inbev alone - that seems incredibly lazy given there are thousands of craft breweries in the UK.

Overall, for the price I paid (£50 in an Amazon Deal) I enjoyed the experience but will look elsewhere if I do the same for 2018.
